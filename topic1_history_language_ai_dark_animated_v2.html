Topic 1

The History of Language AI

AI is the science of building systems that can do things we associate with human intelligence — understand, reason, and learn.

The idea has been around for decades — the term “AI” dates back to the 1950s.

ChatGPT: 1 million users in 5 days

Language AI went mainstream in late 2022.

Netflix: ~3.5 years
Instagram: ~2.5 months
ChatGPT: 5 days

Let’s rewind and see how we got here.

A Brief History of Language AI

Colors: Non-transformer Embeddings Attention Encoder-only Decoder-only Encoder–decoder

Bag-of-words (early 2000s)

Idea (1 sentence): Represent a document as a “bag” of word counts — order is ignored.

Limitation: No sense of meaning or order — “dog bites man” ≈ “man bites dog.”

→ We needed a way to capture context and meaning.

word2vec (2013)

Idea: Use neural networks to turn words into numbers called embeddings so similar words map to nearby points.

Limitation: Meaning of a word can change with context and it struggled with long sentences.

→ We needed a way to focus on the relevant words in each sentence.

Attention (2017)

Idea: Let the model place a spotlight on the most important words while reading or writing.

Breakthrough: “Attention Is All You Need” (2017) introduced the Transformer — the architecture behind modern LLMs including ChatGPT.

→ With Transformers, language systems became far more fluent and capable.

From Transformers to ChatGPT

  • BERT (2018): understands context by reading in both directions (good for understanding).
  • GPT family (2018 →): excels at predicting the next word (good for generation).
  • T5 / Flan‑T5 & Switch: flexible “text‑to‑text” learners (instruction following).
  • ChatGPT (2022): made these capabilities accessible to everyone.

Summary

Bag‑of‑words → counts words
word2vec → maps meaning with vectors
Attention/Transformers → understand context

This evolution enabled today’s LLMs like ChatGPT.
